Launch Hoboken Innovation Portal

My Role: UI Designer; Type of Project: Competition; Software: Figma


Project Description: The City of Hoboken's recently completed Innovation Center Feasibility Study maps out the potential for building a robust, coordinated, and equitable innovation ecosystem in Hoboken. One of the study's key recommendations is an online Innovation Portal, to serve as a "one-stop-shop" for innovation and entrepreneurial activity, resources, and contacts. To kick off the implementation of the study's recommendations, Stevens Venture Center, in collaboration with the City, is calling on future engineers, designers, programmers, and entrepreneurs to build a prototype web user interface for the future Innovation Portal.

My Timeline: March 24, 2021 - March 31, 2021



The following pages I designed:

  • Placeholder page with Newsletter Subscription CTA
  • Landing Page
  • News Page
  • Calendar Page
  • Discussion Page
  • Event Submission Form
  • Inquiry Submission Form
  • Sign In Page
  • Registration Page

Inspiration

The logo for Launch Hoboken was a big inspiration for my design. I wanted to contribute to the branding of the portal by using the rectangular vector geometry thoughout the pages. I was also inspired by how the portal wants to provide ease for its users in finding entrepreneurial and innovative projects going on in Hoboken. Ease to me best reflects lighter colors such as the light blue in the gradient background that fades into white.


First Interation: Finalist




What I learned

Unfortunately, my submission did not win but as a finalist, I got to interview with the judges at Launch Hoboken and implement the following from their feedback:



Confirmation alerts for the forms:




I addressed the judges' concern about users submitting events and expecting any event to go through without approval. I implemented alerts for the forms to notify users that their submissions are being processed by using the overlay option in prototyping on Figma. Specifically, for the event form, users will be notified that their event will be reviewed by the Launch Hoboken team.

More Calls to Action (CTAs) in the Landing Page:




I addressed the critique about users possibly not being drawn enough into the landing page due to the lack of images and CTAs. I made accessing the other pages easier and more engaging on the landing page by using pictures and overlayed text to communicate various actions that users can do, such as viewing the news, seeing events on the calendar, and joining discussions.

Second Interation

I decided to also change the shades of blue I used throughout the project to enhance the more inviting feel of the UI. Overall, the project was a great experience that I am happy to share!
